# Vandan Patel Portfolio — Design Craft Audit

**Audited:** 2026-07-21 · working tree at commit `dafe22e` ("updates night fury"), dev build served at `http://localhost:3111/~vpatel4/portfolio`.

**Method.** Judged against the **Awwwards studio-portfolio tier** the DESIGN_PLAN freezes as the bar — Cuberto / Lusion / Dennis Snellenberg. Capture matrix: single-page site (`Home/Hero → About → Experience → Projects → Skills → Journeys(3D tunnel) → Contact → Footer`) at **1440×900** and **390×844**, dark theme (the only theme), plus overlay-menu-open, preloader, reduced-motion, and hover/focus-visible states. Contrast figures are **computed WCAG ratios** (luminance from `getComputedStyle`), not eyeballed; all type sizes, paddings and element rects are computed values read live from the running DOM. This audit weights the user's core complaint — the site *accreted* features without a plan and reads as "not well put together" — so cohesion (ambient-layer collisions, narrative seams, one type system, dosage per scroll depth) is scored as heavily as classic craft defects.

**Caveats.** The overlay-nav click bug was fixed immediately before this pass and is *excluded* from findings (spot-verified: a menu destination scrolls correctly). Playwright MutationObserver/favicon console noise and jest `act()` warnings are excluded. Measurements were taken at a 1200/1440-wide viewport; heading sizes below the clamp cap shift slightly with width (noted where relevant). `src/` was read-only for this audit; no source was modified.

## 1. Top 15 issues, ranked

| # | Sev | Page | Problem | Suggested fix |
|---|-----|------|---------|---------------|
| 1 | S2 | Skills / Projects (ambient) | **Aurora orb lands untethered on live content at multiple scroll depths.** The orb is `position: fixed` and driven by scroll/cursor (`AuroraThread.css:130`, `translate3d`); at Skills depth its glow sits over the "SQL" skill text (~x710,y495) and at Projects depth it floats in the whitespace beside the "Projects" heading (~x710,y328). A decorative beam that is meant to *thread* the page instead collides with and distracts from content — the single most visible symptom of the "accreted, not composed" complaint. | Constrain the orb to the margin/gutter rail (never enter the content column); or gate its opacity down to ~0.15 whenever it overlaps a text node; anchor its path to the aurora beam (`left:50%`) instead of free cursor tracking. |
| 2 | S2 | Whole page (ambient dosage) | **Too many ambient systems fire at the same scroll depth.** At the Projects/Skills fold the page runs simultaneously: fixed dragon poster (`app-dragon-poster`, z0), aurora beam + orb (`AuroraThread`, z0), film grain overlay, and the section content — with the marquee and 3D tunnel arriving moments later. No scroll depth gets a single dominant element; the ambient layer competes with itself. | Define a dosage budget: at most ONE ambient motion system dominant per fold. Fade the dragon poster out before Projects, hold the aurora as a thin static thread through editorial sections, and let the 3D tunnel own its fold alone. |
| 3 | S2 | Global type | **Section-heading scale has no modular logic and the hierarchy is inverted.** Computed h-levels: Hero 168px/700, Contact 80px/400, Projects 72px/400, About 45.6px/400, Skills 36px/400 (`HeroSection.css:68`, `ContactSection.css:20`, `ProjectsSection.css:23`, `AboutSection.css:22`, `SkillsSection.css:16`). Step ratios are 2.1 / 1.11 / 1.58 / 1.27 — no consistent scale. Worse, the *least* informative heading (single word "Projects", 72px) is 2× the *most* informative (full sentence "Tools and concepts I reach for…", 36px). | Adopt one modular scale (e.g. 1.25 ratio) with named roles: `display` (hero only), `section-title`, `section-lede`. Decide sentence-headings vs word-headings as a rule and cap sentence-headings so they never fall below the word-headings. |
| 4 | S2 | Narrative arc | **Abrupt mode-switches break the arc.** The page flips register four times: 3D/WebGL hero → flat editorial (About/Experience/Projects/Skills) → 3D pinned tunnel (Journeys) → flat editorial (Contact). The two 3D moments bookend nothing and the tunnel arrives with no transition scaffolding, so it reads as a bolted-on feature rather than a beat in a story. | Add connective tissue: a shared transition (grain wipe / color shift) into and out of the tunnel, and echo one hero motion motif at Contact so the 3D bookends feel intentional. |
| 5 | S2 | Projects (ambient) | **Dragon recede leaves an awkward half-off-screen silhouette at Projects depth.** The recede choreography parks the dragon poster partially clipped on the right edge mid-page — a partial silhouette that reads as a rendering glitch, not composition. | Choreograph the dragon to fully exit (translate past the viewport or fade to 0) before the Projects fold, or dock it deliberately at a composed crop. |
| 6 | S3 | Global type | **Heading weight is inconsistent** — Hero is 700 (bold) while every other section heading is 400 (`fw` measured). Only the name carries weight, so sections share no repeated "this is a heading" identity; the eye can't lock a pattern. | Give section titles a single shared weight (e.g. 600) distinct from body; reserve 700 for the hero display alone, as a deliberate one-off. |
| 7 | S3 | Global type | **Sentence-as-heading vs word-as-heading mixed with no rule.** About and Skills use full sentences as their h2 ("I care about software that is useful…", "Tools and concepts I reach for…"); Projects/Contact use short phrases. There is no principle for what a section heading *is*. | Pick one heading grammar per level — short label headings with a separate lede paragraph is the studio-tier convention. |
| 8 | S3 | Global rhythm | **Inner-block vertical rhythm drifts** even though outer section padding is uniform (120px — see do-not-regress). Component internal paddings scatter with no scale: Journeys `6px` (`JourneysSection.css:26`), About `16px` (`AboutSection.css:63`), Hero `28px` (`HeroSection.css:116`), Experience `36px` (`ExperienceSection.css:15`), Projects `52px` (`ProjectsSection.css:16`). | Snap all internal spacing to a 4/8px scale with ~3 named steps; the 120px outer rhythm is already correct and should be the anchor. |
| 9 | S3 | Meta / labels | **Secondary meta-label tier drifts** while the eyebrow tier is systematized. Eyebrows are consistent (12px / 600 / 2.4px tracking) but meta labels are not: `hero__meta` 14px/550/normal vs `hero__stat-label` 13px/550/normal vs `site-toggle__label` 13px/600/1.82px. | Define one meta-label token (size + weight + tracking) and apply it everywhere secondary labels appear. |
| 10 | S3 | Body copy legibility | **Smallest muted copy sits at 12px + white-alpha 0.5** (`color: rgba(255,255,255,.5)`, `fs:12`), the lowest-contrast, smallest text on the page. Full-opacity text is excellent (17.66:1) but composited this muted tier is ~5:1 — a thin margin at 12px. | Lift muted text to alpha ≥0.62 or size ≥14px so the muted tier clears 4.5:1 with margin. |

| 11 | S2 | Journeys seam | **Three systems collide at the Skills→Journeys seam.** In one viewport: the aurora orb sits dead-center in the whitespace between the marquee and the "Hiking & travelling" heading, the dragon is clipped at BOTH the right edge and bottom-left corner simultaneously, and the ghost-outline marquee ("EXPLORE · REPEAT") introduces a third competing display-type register at near-invisible contrast. The page's worst single fold for the "accreted, not composed" complaint. | One owner per fold: fully retire the dragon before the marquee; dock the orb to the beam; either commit to the ghost marquee as a designed divider (raise stroke contrast, tie size to hero scale) or cut it. |
| 12 | S3 | Contact | **The close is half-empty.** Right ~45% of the Contact fold holds only a clipped black/red dragon fragment on the far edge (`contact_1440_dark_fold.jpeg`); all content is left-column. For the final beat of the page, the composition reads unfinished rather than intentionally sparse. | Either compose the dragon fully into the right half at Contact (its "landing" moment — closes the narrative arc) or center the close full-bleed and drop the fragment. |
| 13 | S3 | Skills→Journeys divider | **Marquee strip is a third display-type system.** `clamp(3rem,11vw,9rem)` outline type at near-invisible stroke contrast, unrelated to the hero scale or the section-title scale, `line-height:1` risks descender clipping. Decorative divider with no size/contrast relationship to the rest of the type system. | Tie marquee size to the hero display token, raise the outline stroke to a deliberate low-but-legible contrast (~2.5–3:1 decorative), or delete the strip. |
| 14 | S3 | Mobile ambient dosage | **The ambient stack persists on 390px.** The 24px aurora orb + beam + grain + dragon/poster all remain active on mobile (`AuroraThread.css` mobile block keeps the orb by choice); on a 390×844 canvas the ambient layer occupies proportionally far more attention against far less content width. Structural reflow itself is clean (see do-not-regress). | On ≤640px: keep ONE ambient element (beam at reduced glow), hide the orb, and fade the dragon/poster to a hero-only presence. |
| 15 | S3 | Preloader | **Intro motion runs ~1.77s total** (opaque gate 1.14s + 0.63s curtain, measured) against the ≤1.5s web-alive guideline; combined with the hero SplitText sequence (~1.4s) a first-time visitor waits ~3s before the page feels interactive. Once-per-session guard mitigates but the first impression carries the cost. | Trim opaque gate to ~0.9s (fonts.ready typically beats it) and overlap the curtain with the hero reveal more aggressively; keep total ≤1.5s. |

**Near-misses worth fixing while in there.** Marquee strip type (`clamp(3rem,11vw,9rem)`) is another display-size system competing with the hero — consider tying it to the hero scale. The 12px eyebrow tracking (2.4px) is slightly wide for the size. `line-height:1` on the marquee risks clipping descenders at large sizes.

## 2. What's already good — do not regress

- **Text contrast is best-in-class.** Every full-opacity text sample computes **≥17.66:1** (nav links `rgb(242,242,240)` 17.66:1; headings 17.66:1) against the near-black canvas — comfortably beyond WCAG AAA (7:1). Do not darken text or lighten the canvas.
- **Outer section rhythm is uniform and correct.** Every `.content-section` uses a consistent **120px top / 120px bottom** shell (measured on About/Experience/Projects/Skills/Journeys/Contact). This is the one structural thing that already reads as "composed" — preserve it as the spacing anchor.
- **Eyebrow/kicker system is consistent.** Uppercase eyebrows are uniformly **12px / weight 600 / 2.4px tracking** across Hero, About, Experience and tags — a real, repeated micro-system. Keep it.
- **Single serif display family is coherent.** All headings use **Fraunces**; body/meta use **Manrope** — a clean two-family pairing. The problem is the *scale*, not the families.
- **`prefers-reduced-motion` is respected broadly** — reduce blocks exist in `HeroSection.css:25`, `AuroraThread.css:224`, `JourneysSection.css:130`, `JourneyTunnel.css:79`, `MarqueeStrip.css:60`, `ProjectsSection.css:127`, `OverlayNav.css:162`, `CursorFollower.css:53`, and `Preloader.js` skips the intro entirely under reduce. (Visually confirmed below.)
- **Ambient layers are correctly z-ordered behind content** (`AuroraThread` documented z-index contract, content z1) so collisions are visual-glow only, never occlusion.
- **Overlay menu a11y contract is complete and keyboard-verified** — focus-in on open, Tab trap wraps both directions, Esc closes with focus returned to toggle, background `inert`, Lenis scroll-lock. Verified twice by independent agents.
- **Focus-visible rings are systematic** — 2px solid `#EF4444`, 3px offset, present on all sampled interactive elements (brand, toggle, CTAs, project links).
- **390px structural reflow is clean** — no horizontal overflow (measured), targets ≥44px, hero stats 2×2, tunnel correctly replaced by the marquee fallback below 860px.
- **Journeys 3D tunnel runs at 61fps** with clean pin/unpin both directions, no spacer leaks, and a full capability-gate + marquee fallback chain.
- **Reduced-motion support is genuinely complete** — preloader skipped, splits static, marquees become scrollable rows, orb hidden, WebGL never initializes, poster fallback art shows. Verified end-to-end three times.
